## Purpose

This policy defines "baseline" control measures for securing information on {{ config.extra.organization }}’ computer networks. Information security and privacy is a team effort requiring the participation of every team member who encounters {{ config.extra.organization }} information and/or information systems.

Everyone at {{ config.extra.organization }} is expected to be familiar with, and consistently comply with, these policies. These security measures are the minimum required to prevent a variety of different problems that could occur within {{ config.extra.organization }}, including fraud, embezzlement, industrial espionage, sabotage, reputation risk, compliancy risk, errors and omissions, as well as information systems unavailability. These policies also outline the minimum controls necessary to prevent legal recourse such as allegations of {{ config.extra.organization }} negligence, data loss, or privacy violations. The Information Technology Department considered these threats and others during the preparation of these policies; the result of their efforts is this policy document detailing reasonable and practical ways for all of us at {{ config.extra.organization }} to prevent unnecessary losses.

### Audience and Overview

This Information Security Policy applies to all employees and contractors of {{ config.extra.organization }} data. We know this is a lot of content, but you are expected to read and comply with each of these policy elements. A summary of this content could be described in this overview:

- Be always professional; Company reputation is important.
- Protect data, whether it belongs to you, your coworkers, or customers.
- Protect equipment, intellectual property, and other things owned by {{ config.extra.organization }}.
- Your account and password are yours and yours alone - no one else has a right to it.
- If you see something suspicious that might harm {{ config.extra.organization }} - whether in the office, or email, or online - say something to someone, even privately, to make sure that it's OK, or to confirm that it's suspicious and needs to be addressed.
- Respect Company and other people’s equipment, data, or ideas.
- Much of this policy will concern details to continuing earning and keeping our customers’ trust. We have several regulations with which we must comply ISO 27001, ISO 27701, SOC 2 and other certifications to maintain.

This document intends to support the Company’s reputation for high-integrity and high-quality business dealings.

## Non-Public Personal Information Privacy, PII, ePHI

We handle private customer data; therefore, {{ config.extra.organization }} takes reasonable measures to ensure the protection of privacy and private information stored within {{ config.extra.organization }} systems. This data could include names, addresses, SSNs, ePHI, and payment card information, just to name a few or any combination of data that could uniquely identify an individual (Personal Identifiable Information [PII]).
{{ config.extra.organization }} continually strives to maintain privacy and security standards according to HIPAA (health PHI) GDPR (EU residents), GLBA (financial), and FERPA (education).

Access controls and restrictions have been implemented to prevent unauthorized access to customer confidential information (e.g., ePHI, PII), employee confidential information, and various other confidential data stored on {{ config.extra.organization }} networks.

To facilitate the protection of the Confidential Information and our mutual business Interests in the success of {{ config.extra.organization }}, you agree that, during your employment with us, you will not, directly or indirectly, render any services or other assistance to hold any financial interest in, or accept any gifts or other compensation of any kind from, any actual or prospective competitor of {{ config.extra.organization }}.

You further agree to comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including those related to anti-corruption, anti-bribery, and anti-money laundering. You agree to report any known or suspected violations of these laws and regulations to the appropriate {{ config.extra.organization }} personnel.

During your employment with {{ config.extra.organization }}, you may have gratned access to, or be entrusted with, confidential information, including but not limited to, trade secrets, proprietary information, and other confidential information of {{ config.extra.organization }}, its customers, clients, employees, or partners. You agree to hold all such information in strict confidence and not to disclose it to any third party, or use it for any purpose other than the performance of your duties for {{ config.extra.organization }}, without the prior written consent of {{ config.extra.organization }}.

Encryption must be used whenever sensitive information (e.g., ePHI, SSN) is sent over a public network like the Internet or email. Encryption should also be used when storing Internal or Confidential data on machines that may leave {{ config.extra.organization }} premises.

All paper copies of sensitive information (e.g., SSNs, PHI, payment card information) must be disposed of immediately consistent with proper destruction protocols. Please consult your supervisor or the Information Technology Department with specific questions.

You should also be aware that information transmitted through email and the Internet is not entirely secure or may contain viruses or malware. The information you transmit and receive could damage the Company's systems and reputation or competitiveness. Violations of this policy may result in termination for a first offense.

Internal and external emails are considered business records and may be subject to federal and state recordkeeping requirements and discovery in the event of litigation. Be aware of this possibility when sending emails within and outside the Company.

All use of Company-provided communications systems, including email and internet use, should conform to our guidelines/policies, including but not limited to Equal Opportunity, Harassment, Confidential Information, and Conflicts of Interest. For example, {{ config.extra.organization }} employees should not engage in harassing or discriminatory behavior that targets other {{ config.extra.organization }} employees or individuals because of their protected class status or defamatory comments.

Because email, telephone, voice mail, and internet communication equipment are provided for business purposes and are critical to its success, your communications may be accessed without further notice by The Company to ensure compliance with this guideline.

The electronic communication systems are not secure and may allow inadvertent disclosure or accidental transmission to third parties. Therefore, sensitive information should not be sent via unsecured electronic means.

Sensitive information (see [Data Classifications](#data-classifications) section) should not leave {{ config.extra.organization }} the office or its associated systems without both writen authorization and approval from senior management.

Use of software licensed to {{ config.extra.organization }} on a personal computer owned by a User is not authorized unless the system has been designated a system that is used to process {{ config.extra.organization }} information. Use of a personal computer to process {{ config.extra.organization }} information must be approved by the Information Technology Department (see also: [BYOD Policy](#bring-your-own-device-policy-byod)).

All information stored, transmitted, received, or contained during using the Internet on {{ config.extra.organization }}'s systems is the property of {{ config.extra.organization }}, which reserves the right to review and analyze Internet usage. Such information may be used to determine if any User is violating {{ config.extra.organization }}'s policy.

Access to the Internet may be revoked in whole or in part, without prior notice or cause; we also reserve the right to restrict access to certain sites in the sole discretion of management.

The following activities are strictly prohibited, with no exceptions:

- Violations of the rights of any person or Company protected by copyright, trade secret, patent or other intellectual property, or similar laws or regulations, including, but not limited to, the installation or distribution of "pirated" or other software products that are not appropriately licensed for use by {{ config.extra.organization }}.
- Unauthorized copying of copyrighted material including, but not limited to, digitization and distribution of photographs from magazines, books or other copyrighted sources, copyrighted music, and the installation of any copyrighted software for which {{ config.extra.organization }} or the end user does not have an active license is strictly prohibited.
- Exporting software, technical information, encryption software or technology, in violation of international or regional export control laws, is illegal. The appropriate management should be consulted prior to the export of any material that is in question.
- Knowingly introducing malicious programs into the network or server (e.g., viruses, worms, Trojan horses, etc.).
- Revealing {{ config.extra.organization }} account passwords to others or allowing others usage of an account not their own. This includes family and other household members when work is being done at home.
- Using a {{ config.extra.organization }} computing asset to actively engage in procuring or transmitting material that is in violation of any local, state, and federal laws.
- Making fraudulent offers of products, items, or services originating from any {{ config.extra.organization }} account.
- Effecting security breaches or disruptions of network communication. Security breaches include, but are not limited to, accessing data of which the employee is not an intended recipient or logging into a server or account that the employee is not expressly authorized to access, unless these duties are within the scope of regular duties. For purposes of this section, "disruption" includes, but is not limited to, network sniffing, pinged floods, packet spoofing, denial of service, and forged routing information for malicious purposes.
- Port scanning, security testing, or security scanning is expressly prohibited unless prior notification to and authorization by {{ config.extra.organization }} is made.
- Executing any form of network monitoring which will intercept data not intended for the employee's host, port or security scanning, or security testing intended to compromise or breach the confidentiality, integrity or availability of {{ config.extra.organization }}’ systems, unless this activity is a part of the employee's normal job/duty and authorized.
- Circumventing user authentication or security of any host, network, or account.
- Interfering with or denying service to any user other than the employee's host (e.g., denial of service attack)
- Using any program/script/command, or sending messages of any kind, with the intent to interfere with, or disable, a user's session, via any means, locally or via the Internet/Intranet.
- Providing information about, or lists of, {{ config.extra.organization }} customers and employees to parties outside {{ config.extra.organization }} unless it is a part of normal job duties.

### Removable Media

Users must not store {{ config.extra.organization }}’ Confidential data at any time on removable devices (e.g., USB drives, CDs). No confidential customer data (PII, ePHI) may be stored on removable media at any time.

Sensitive data may be copied or stored on removable media only by authorized users in the performance of official duties. Media containing information shall be protected against unauthorized access, misuse, or corruption. When in transit, Sensitive data stored on removable media must not be left unattended and must always remain in an authorized employee’s physical control.

Physical controls shall be provided for removable media containing sensitive information. Staff shall adhere to the requirements of the applicable disposal policy and procedures when decommissioning removable media. Removable media shall be kept in a secure safe or a locked cabinet and returned to safe storage at the end of each workday.

For the purposes of definition, the following items shall fall under the category of removable media:

- Flash (Jump) Drives and flash memory storage
- SD Storage
- Removable fixed drives and portable caddies
- R/W Compact Disc or DVD media
- USB storage devices

### Passwords

Choose difficult-to-guess passwords. Passwords must NOT be words in the dictionary and must not reflect the User's personal information.

For example, spouse's name, pet’s name, or birth dates are unacceptable passwords. Strong passwords are easier to remember when memory techniques are employed; for example, familiar words can be used by replacing an "a" with an "@" symbol, or an "i" with an "!".

All user passwords must be at least eight characters in length.
Choose passwords that include the four following characters:

1. Alpha- uppercase
1. Alpha- lowercase
1. Numeric
1. Special Characters ($, \*, @, etc.)

#### Changing Passwords

To help counter the possibility of a password compromise, all user passwords must be changed on a regular basis. Passwords must be changed every 90 days. User passwords must not be reused or recycled when changed. Switching between two, or a similar small number of, passwords is prohibited.

Where systems support it, user passwords are forced to change every 90 days. Where systems support it, expired passwords will be employed. Expired passwords are passwords, which must be changed after the first time they are used. If you suspect that somebody else may know your password, your password must be changed immediately.

#### Protecting Accounts and Passwords

If your password is stolen or otherwise compromised, it could be used to masquerade as you on the network. Therefore, protect your passwords. Passwords are inherently at risk because the same password can be used repeatedly. It is critical that your password is known only by you, and the Information Technology Department only when necessary. If you must share your password with a member of the Information Technology Department, it must be changed as soon as possible thereafter.

Do not store your passwords in any computer files (e.g., spreadsheet). Likewise, an employee’s password must not be written down, sent by email, or shared over the phone with anyone.

Do not opt to save passwords on websites for auto entry purposes. Instead, use an IT recommended password vault manager.

## Bring Your Own Device Policy (BYOD)

The purpose of this BYOD policy is to set out standards, guidelines, and policy to ensure that Company information, customer data, business communications, and trade secrets are not at risk when present on an employee’s personal device.

This policy is intended to protect the security and integrity of {{ config.extra.organization }}’ data and technology infrastructure. Limited exceptions to the policy may occur due to variations in devices and platforms. {{ config.extra.organization }} employees must agree to the terms and conditions set forth in this policy to be able to connect their devices to the Company network.

### BYOD Acceptable Use Policy

The Company defines acceptable business use as activities that directly or indirectly support the business of {{ config.extra.organization }}. The Company defines acceptable personal use on Company time as reasonable and limited personal communication or recreation, such as reading or game playing.

Personal Devices may not be used at any time to:

- Store or transmit {{ config.extra.organization }}’ production data (e.g., corporate proprietary information, ePHI, PII).
- Store or transmit illicit materials via {{ config.extra.organization }} networks.
- Store or transmit proprietary information belonging to another Company.

Employees may use their mobile device to access the following Company-owned resources: email, calendars, contacts, and documents. Employees may not use cloud-based apps or backup that allows Company-related data to be transferred to unsecured parties or stored in personal storage.

### BYOD Devices and Support

Devices we help you support include items such as smartphones and tablets. Connectivity issues are not supported by IT. macOS devices, even {{ config.extra.organization }}-owned Macs, are not officially supported, but are supported on an as-can basis. While {{ config.extra.organization }} IT will make reasonable efforts to protect {{ config.extra.organization }}-owned Mac computers, use of a Mac computer on the {{ config.extra.organization }} network implies the user’s personal liability for the user’s actions and any negative impact on the network.

Requests for reimbursements for devices and accessories should be directed to your manager.

### BYOD Security

As a condition of connecting a personal device to the Company network, the device must be secured. To help prevent unauthorized access, devices must be protected by PIN or password. To prevent accidental disclosure of information, the device must be encrypted, and antivirus/antimalware must be installed.

To improve security, we recommend the following:

- The device should lock itself with a password or PIN if it’s idle for five minutes.
- After five failed login attempts, the device should lock.
- Only install apps from trusted sources (e.g., App Store or Google Play).

### BYOD Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication (2FA) is required for all {{ config.extra.organization }} employee accounts. This is especially important for employees who use their personal devices to access {{ config.extra.organization }} resources. As a result, you may be asked to install an authenticator app on your personal device. If this is not personally acceptable, the employee is responsible for providing an alternative device for 2FA. Please speak with your manager if you have any concerns.

### BYOD Risks/Liabilities/Disclaimers

The Company reserves the right to disconnect devices or disable services without notification. Employees are responsible for notifying their mobile carrier upon loss of a device. Employees are expected to always use their devices in an ethical manner and adhere to the Company’s acceptable use policy as outlined above.

Employees are personally liable for all costs associated with their devices. {{ config.extra.organization }} reserves the right to take appropriate disciplinary action up to and including termination for noncompliance with this policy.

### Data Classifications

We use three sensitivity classifications: **Public**, **Sensitive**, and **Confidential**. Below are definitions, labels, restrictions levels, and examples of each.

If you are uncertain of how a particular piece of information should be classified, please direct your questions to IT. By default, unlabeled information should be considered Sensitive.

#### Public

Public information may be disclosed to anyone without violating an individual's rights to privacy. Knowledge of this information does not expose confidential or sensitive information.

##### Example

Marketing brochures, Website, Business cards, Press Releases Reproduction: Unless otherwise stated, Public information may be reproduced

- Handling (retention and destruction): No restrictions.
- Labeling: Public documents should be marked as Public.

#### Sensitive

Sensitive information may only be disclosed to {{ config.extra.organization }} employees and personnel covered by non-disclosure agreement. Unauthorized disclosure, compromise, or use of information could pose minimal impact to {{ config.extra.organization }}, its customers, or employees.

##### Example

Employee Handbook, phone directory, organization charts, policies, routine administrative and office information, product source code, unreleased products, etc.

- Reproduction: Reproduction requires authorization.
- Handling (retention and destruction): Access is limited to internal employees. Stored on internal repositories or approved cloud providers.
- Labeling: Sensitive documents must be marked as Sensitive - for In Use Only on each page. Distribution requires signed mutual non-disclosure agreement (MNDA).

#### Confidential

Confidential information is information whose unauthorized disclosure, compromise, or destruction could have an adverse impact on {{ config.extra.organization }}, its customer, or employees and could damage {{ config.extra.organization }}'s reputation, cause loss of business, or result in potential legal action from disclosure. Confidential information is intended solely for use within {{ config.extra.organization }} with a specific need to know.

##### Example

System requirements, personnel records, client records, financial plans, client correspondence, budget information, security plans and standards, system data, and network services such as Active Directory.

- Reproduction: Reproduction is prohibited.
- Access: Access is restricted on a need-to-know basis. Must be stored on internal repositories or approved cloud providers and encrypted.
- Handling (retention and destruction): Access is restricted on a need-to-know basis. Stored on 1. internal repositories or approved cloud providers, and 2. In encrypted format
- Labeling: Confidential files are to be labeled as such on each page. Distribution requires sign MNDA.

Additionally, they help prevent viruses and malicious code damage.

These mechanisms include VPN and firewalls (including workstation, corporate, and perimeter). Access via VPN is granted by the IT.

Prior to connecting to {{ config.extra.organization }} internal systems via VPN, LAN connections, etc., any employee or third-party must be approved, authorized, and documented by IT. These privileges must only be enabled for the time required to accomplish previously defined and approved tasks. {{ config.extra.organization }} reserves the right to audit, even terminate, the third-party connections without warning.

Prior to sending any Sensitive or Confidential information to a third-party for any reason, the third-party must sign a {{ config.extra.organization }} mutual non-disclosure agreement (MNDA). Signed MNDAs must be retained, and a copy should be forwarded to the Information Technology Department.

All requests from third parties for information about {{ config.extra.organization }} and its business activities must be referred to management. These requests include press interviews, questionnaires, speeches, etc. This policy does not apply to marketing information about {{ config.extra.organization }} services, nor does it pertain to customer support calls.

Visitors (those who are not {{ config.extra.organization }} employees, authorized contractors, or consultants) must be documented and supervised when visiting offices or other {{ config.extra.organization }} physical locations.
