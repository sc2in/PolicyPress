## Purpose

{{ config.extra.organization }} recognizes the importance and criticality of maintaining a fully functioning Information Security Management System (ISMS) and Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) in accordance with the ISO-27001:2022 and ISO-27701:2019 standards and requirements respectively. In order to properly maintain the ISMS and PIMS, {{ config.extra.organization }} requires an internal audit of systems, documents, and personnel to take place at specified intervals as defined in ISO-27001:2022 section 9.

## Frequency

The internal audit for {{ config.extra.organization }} will take place at a minimum of once per annum. The duration of conducting the information gathering portion of the audit should take no longer than one work week.

## Scope

The internal audit for {{ config.extra.organization }} is limited to those systems, policies and procedures, documents, and departments tasked with handling customer information that resides within the {{ config.extra.organization }}' product or as otherwise declared in the ISMS and PIMS documentation. All physical locations that fall under this scope should be audited on-site. Any departments which fall under this scope should be audited. This includes at least, but is not limited to: Engineering, Technical Support, Human Resources, and Customer Success. 100% of the clauses, ISO-27001:2022 Annex A controls, and ISO-27701:2019 Annex B controls are tested annually.

## Criteria

The criteria for the audit is that all of the following adhere strictly to the ISMS and are followed by those tasked with implementation, maintenance, and daily operation of systems covered by the ISMS and PIMS.

- Internal policies and procedures
- ISMS and PIMS Documentation
- ISMS and PIMS Education and Training
- Additional Information Security criteria required by customer contractual agreements
- 3rd party tools and platforms which implement in part or in whole of the ISMS and PIMS
- Legal Requirements (e.g., GDPR, GLBA, NYDFS)

## Methods

The audit for {{ config.extra.organization }} will consist of three main phases.

### Planning

Planning for a specific audit should take place in advance of the audit. At planning, this document, as well as results from previous audits and any additional scope or criteria should be taken into account. Timing and scheduling should take place at this time.

### Internal Audit

The main internal audit will take place as defined from the planning session. Information gathering will be conducted using the following methods.

#### Reviewing Documentation

Documentation will be reviewed for adherence to the ISO27001:2022 and ISO27701:2019 standards and for consistency with the Statement of Applicability (SOA) and the Risk Treatment Plan (RTP).

#### Observation

Observation of the ISMS in practice should be conducted by the auditor to ensure adherence to the ISO27001:2022 and ISO27701:2019 standards and for consistency with the SOA and the RTP. Samples of information should be taken to prove compliance or non-compliance with these documents.

#### Interviews

Those persons tasked with implementing, maintaining, and the daily operation of the ISMS will be interviewed to ascertain adherence to the ISO27001:2022 and ISO27701:2019 standards and for consistency with the SOA and the RTP. Scheduling for these interviews should be handled at planning and the interviewee allowed time to prepare for the interview. Ad hoc interviews should be avoided when possible.

### Report

Following the internal audit, the auditor will summarize the findings into an audit report that will be delivered for management review. The results of these reviews will be documented and kept on record in accordance with ISO27001:2022 section 9.3.

## Auditor

The auditor for the internal audit will be chosen by leadership in accordance with ISO27001:2022 section 9.2. The auditor should have a deep background in Information Security Management Systems, the ISO27001:2022 standard, and the ISO27701:2019 standard, preferability certified in auditing such systems. If necessary, the auditor may be brought in from a 3rd party accredited organization.
